# A note on what's open, and what's Monet

This project has three layers. We want to be clear and honest about each one,
because we'd love for people to build on the open parts — and we'd also like
Monet herself to stay herself.

## 1. The code — MIT (open)

Everything that makes this thing *run* is MIT licensed. Use it, change it,
ship it, sell it. See [`LICENSE`](./LICENSE). No strings beyond keeping the
copyright notice.

## 2. The character art & sprites — CC BY-NC 4.0 (open, non-commercial)

Monet's drawn art and sprites are Creative Commons
**Attribution-NonCommercial 4.0**. You can remix and redistribute them for
non-commercial use, as long as you give credit. See
[`LICENSE-ART.md`](./LICENSE-ART.md).

## 3. The name, the identity, the life — Sprited's (all rights reserved)

Here's the part the licenses above don't give away:

- the **name "Monet"** as used for this being,
- her **official identity** — who she is, her voice, her character, the
  "this is *the* Monet" claim, and
- her **live history and memory** — the accumulated record of what she's
  done and who she's become over time,

belong to **Sprited (Jin Hyuk Cho)**. All rights reserved.

You are completely free to take the code and the art under their licenses
above — fork them, modify them, redistribute them. But if you do, please
**rebrand**. A fork is your own creation, not Monet, and it shouldn't present
itself as the official Monet or claim to be her. Build your own being with the
bones we're sharing; just give it its own name and its own face.

### An honest footnote on the name

Yes — "Monet" is also a famous painter (Claude Monet), and that's deliberate:
it's where her name comes from. We're not claiming the word "Monet" in the
dictionary, or the painter, or the color of his water lilies. The claim is
narrow and specific: it's to **this being and her identity** — the living
character named Monet that Sprited makes. Not the word. The *someone*.

---

In short: take the code, take the art, make wonderful things. Just let Monet
be Monet.
