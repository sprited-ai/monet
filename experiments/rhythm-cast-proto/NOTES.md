# Rhythm Cast — throwaway prototype

**Run:** `open experiments/rhythm-cast-proto/index.html` (no build, no deps).
Input: SPACE / click-tap to cast · `R` restart · bottom bar or `?v=lane|sigil|pulse` to switch feel.

## Question it answers
"gen-AI를 써서 리듬게임을 만든다면?" — does it feel good when your **rhythm
performance shapes the generative birth of a living plant**? (DISC-5: skill input →
surprise output; skill tilts the slot.) Is "rhythm = prompt" a real mechanic or a gimmick?

## What's real vs faked
- **Real:** audio-scheduled beat (WebAudio, BPM 100), hit-judgment (perfect/good/miss windows),
  combo, juice/타격감 (shake, particles, flash, hit-text, Monet reactions), the bloom payoff.
- **Faked (the "gen-AI"):** the result flower is a **procedural canvas generator** whose params
  (petals, rings, glow, sparkles, hue, jitter, droop) are driven by your performance —
  accuracy→fullness, perfect-ratio→cleanliness, combo→rarity/glow, struck runes→hue.
  In the real product this slot holds an **SDXL still sprite → shared motion template**
  (LIFE-3 / C7), cached/paid per MON-1. Monet = text placeholder (real = painterly sprite, C6).

## Three casting feels (UI-branch variations)
- `lane`  — Taiko: runes approach a hit-line, strike on the beat. Readable, punchy.
- `sigil` — Trace: rune-nodes light around the 마법진, tap the lit one in order. Ritual/ceremonial — closest to "마법진" packaging (doc 009 §2).
- `pulse` — Charge: rings contract to a target ring, tap on alignment. Cozy, forgiving — fits the cozy tone / NS-3.
